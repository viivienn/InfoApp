1. Preparation: 
  • Ensure to have all the proper equipment 
  • Environment is clear from tripping hazard
  • Proper supervision as needed 
  • Keep the patient’s feet pointed towards the direction you would like them to go


    • 2. Positioning principles during transfer
  • Back should be straight. Do not twist or bend your back
  • Feet should be at comfortable shoulder width distance
  • Stay close to the patient
  • Keep your knees slightly bent to help with balance


    • 3. Holding principles during transfer
  • Have a solid grip with fingers closed together 


  • When hand holding patient, wrap your other hand around the patient’s elbow/forearm


  • Encourage the patient to participate 
  • Never
    • Claw grip with hands 
    • Tug on an arm or leg



    • 4. Movement principles during transfer
  • Communicate with the patient about what will be done
  • Perform movements one at a time, step by step
  • Use weight transfer (putting weight from front leg to back leg) to perform movement instead of raw strength in order to reduce risk of injury
    • Putting weight from front leg to back leg as weight transfer technique in order to pull a patient towards the head of the bed


  • Never lift a patient
  • Never push a patient away from you
