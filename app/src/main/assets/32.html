This submodule is not focused on transferring from or to a wheelchair. Please refer to Module 2

General guidelines 
  • Resident seated all the way in the seat (buttocks touching the back of the seat)
  • Knees on a wheelchair at the same level as their buttocks (90° of flexion at the hips and the knees, if possible). Adjust the footrest length if necessary  
  • Resident is sitting straight and not leaning towards either side. If this is not the case, ask for assistance for repositioning 
  • If the resident uses a cushion on their wheelchair, the dip of the cushion faces towards the backrest 


    • To sit resident from walker to wheelchair
    1. Make sure the brakes are on (wheelchair does not move) and foot plates are up
    2. Resident backs up until back of leg is touching the seat
    3. Resident reaches both arms backwards to feel the armrest of the wheelchair
    4. Resident sits down with both arms on the armrest
    5. To avoid sliding: ensure buttocks are touching the back of the seat 

    Sit-to-stand from wheelchair to walker
    1. Make sure brakes are on (wheelchair does not move) and foot plates are up 
    2. Resident pushes up using the armrest, with assistance if needed, until standing position
      • Resident should not pull on the assistant
      • Resident should not hold onto the walker as they stand up (risk of fall)
    3. Resident reaches for walker once in a standing position 

    Additional Information

    When going up a curb :
  • Position front of wheelchair facing the curb 
  • Communicate with resident that the wheelchair will be tilted backwards before going up the curb
  • Tip the wheelchair backwards by pushing on the tipping lever with foot
  • Make sure that front wheels are cleared and onto the curb
  • Roll wheelchairs until rear wheels are touching curb
  • Ease chair forward and roll gently onto curb


    • When going down curb
  • Turn chair around in backward direction
  • Caregiver descent to lower part of curb
  • Gently guide large wheels to lower curb while moving backwards
  • Once large wheels are on lower part, tilt chair back to elevate caster 
  • Continue to move backwards until casted rested on lower part of curb
