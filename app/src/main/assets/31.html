General wheelchair



The brakes
  • Brakes On (wheelchair does not move) when:
    • Resident is sitting in the chair and not planning to go anywhere
    • Transferring residents at all times e.g.from the wheelchair to walker, bed, bath, vice versa. 
  • Brakes Off (wheelchair can move) when resident is well seated and moving

    • The armrests

    • Other components of the wheelchair (only if recommended)
    Follow the guideline for positioning supports, belts, and trays as recommended by the healthcare 

    How to install a wheelchair tray table
    1. Align the tray table with the armrest
    2. Lift the arms of the resident on the tray table 
    3. Glide the tray table through the armrest

    How to install belt:
    1. Have resident to sit back properly 
    2. Buckle the seatbelt 
    3. Tighten seat belt while ensure that one hand can pass between the person and the belt

    Tilt-in-space wheelchair and geriatric wheelchair
  • Tilt adjustment in these wheelchairs provides comfort, stability, positioning and pressure & swelling relief 
  • Geriatic wheelchairs provide increased stabilization with its positioning pads, but require the assistance of another person to mobilize  

    • Special functions: 
  • Tilting of wheelchair 
  • Recline of backrest 
  • Leg readjustment
