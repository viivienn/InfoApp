Residents are very different from one another in age, life expectancy, health condition, and cognition levels. 
  • Many residents are in the end stage of their lives 
  • Even if some may not communicate as much or as easily, it is important to talk with them every day
  • Some residents have no cognitive impairment, while others are deeply affected by them
  • Residents in long term care facilities that are on the younger side may have conditions that decrease their physical abilities, such as multiple sclerosis (MS) or amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS, Lou Gehrig’s disease)
