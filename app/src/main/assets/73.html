• Cognitive impairments affect different brain functions, including memory, logical reasoning, comprehension, and judgement
    • Other signs related to cognitive impairments include problems with language, difficulties performing familiar tasks, and decreased alertness and orientation
  • They can occur naturally with aging, or can be due to a head injury or a condition
  • Cognitive difficulties can be temporary (e.g. delirium) or permanent

    •  

  • Examples of conditions you might observe in long-term care facilities that lead to cognitive impairments include:
    • Alzheimer’s disorder, dementia
    • Schizophrenia
    • Anxiety, depression
