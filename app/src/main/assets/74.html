Residents with cognitive impairments may have:
  • Difficulties following instructions and orders
  • Difficulties performing daily tasks, such as brushing their hair, washing their hands, etc. 
  • Difficulties with memory and attention
  • Decrease in enthusiasm or initiative

    • We must take these difficulties into consideration when we communicate with these residents:
  • When giving instructions, make sure to dictate one step at a time. Avoid giving the instructions all at one, because the resident might have difficulties remembering.
  • The instructions must be simple. Use simpler words if possible.
    • Sometimes, the resident is physically able to perform the movement, but is unable to understand what they are supposed to do. 
  • Make sure to adapt the level of difficulty of the tasks to the resident’s abilities.
    • Demonstrate the action needed to be done to ensure their comprehension. By doing so, the resident will accomplish what is asked of them, which can increase their self-esteem. 
  • If the resident is able to participate in their daily tasks, provide supervision discreetly
    • It sends the message that you trust their abilities to complete a task. 
    • If the resident is unable to complete their task, calmly intervene
  • Validate the resident’s needs and emotions by repeating what they just said
    • Even if you do not understand what the resident tried to express, repeating what they just said can help reduce the resident’s anxiety
