Dysphagia

Swallowing difficulties - takes more time/effort to bring the food or drink from mouth to stomach 
  • Can lead to aspiration (food/liquid entering the airway) which can cause health complications such as pneumonia 
    • ⛔Do not feed residents with dysphagia, ask for a professional. 
    ⛔If you suspect that your resident has dysphagia, ask for a professional.

    Warning Signs of Dysphagia

  • Coughing or choking 
  • Difficulty chewing or chewing for a very long time
  • Accumulation/pocketing of food in the mouth (food not completely swallowed)
  • Regurgitation (bringing food back up)
  • Reflux/vomit 
  • Sound gurgly or wet after swallowing
  • Spill or drooling of food
