Preparation prior to meal:

  • Ensure resident is awake and alert, wearing sensory aids (hearing aids, glasses, dentures)
  • Wash resident’s hands and brush their teeth/dentures before and after meal
  • Position resident for safety and comfort (see submodule 2)
  • Offer resident cloth, napkins or aprons, if needed
  • Ensure the meal correspond to their meal coupon (contains information on their allergies, restrictions and modified textures)
  • Food should be placed in front of resident within reach 
    • If patient is hemiplegic (weakness or paralysis of one side): place food on good side and/or feed towards unaffected cheek
  • Open lid of small containers (e.g. puree) or cut food into smaller bites

    • Assistance during the meal

    The level of assistance provided may vary from verbal encouragement, help cutting food, partial assistance, hand over hand assistance or full assistance. 

  • Sit at resident’s eye level and preferably in front of them or slightly to the side
  • Encourage independent eating (eating by themselves as much as possible)
  • Allow resident to make their own mealtime decisions as much as possible 
  • Encourage the resident to take all fluids provided at and between meals
    • Hydration is key in this population!
    Inadequate fluid intake often leads to prolonged wound healing, constipation, falls and confusion 
  • Make sure that 75% of the meal is eaten, if not, advise a professional.
    • The order does not matter as long it is eaten! (E.g. dessert before appetizer)

    When feeding

  • Make sure the food temperature is appropriate
  • If needed, remind them to not talk when eating 
    • E.g. I am listening to you, you can continue talking after this bite
  • Approach the spoon in a bottom-up motion. Avoid tilting the resident's head backward
  • Ensure the mouth is empty before offering liquid. Avoid mixing liquid and solid together
  • Offer smaller spoonful quantities (size of a full teaspoon)
  • Adjust rate of feeding and bite-size to the person’s
  • Provide reminders to open mouth, chew and swallow, if needed
  • Conversational tips: Name the food that is you offer with a positive attitude enthusiasm 
    • E.g. Let’s move onto the mashed potatoes, they look so good! 

    • After meal

  • Allow the resident to remain seated for at least 30 minutes following the meal
  • Verify that resident has their belongings (e.g. sensory aids) and check the tray before removal 
  • Brush teeth or denture before and after each meal
    • Proper dental hygiene following meal to decrease the risk of dysphagia
