Positioning aids (if necessary)

Pillows

  • Can be used to support different body parts 
  • Flexible: can be rolled, folded, tucked 

Wedges

  • Used when the resident is side-lying
  • Can be used anywhere to provide comfort and a proper alignment

Towels

  • Can be used to support resident’s forearm and hand 
  • Use soft towels to avoid irritating the skin 

Face cloths

  • Can be rolled up to fit inside the palm of the hand 
  • Prevents resident from forming a tight fist 

Changing position 

In the wheelchair:

  • Unless specified otherwise, encourage the resident to relieve pressure from their buttocks by leaning sideways and upwards by supporting themselves using the armrests 

    • In the bed:

  • It is important to change the resident’s position at least every 2h
  • Keep head of bed low (less than 30°) except for short periods of time and meal times

  • Unless specified otherwise, encourage the resident to move themselves frequently using the rails of the bed or by moving their limbs
  • Sequence of position change should follow: back →  right side lying → left side lying → back → etc.

    • Avoid left →  back →  right →  back →  left →  back, as this would increase the amount of time the resident spends lying on their back

    Other methods of preventions

    Regarding during patient handling

  • Minimize shear or friction of the body against the surface of the bed or chair
  • Gently lift limbs and use natural movement to move them
    • Avoid putting or dragging the limbs across the bed

    Regarding humidity

  • Dry out skin properly using patting motion after a shower 
  • Wet bed sheets are change immediately
  • Wet diapers and undergarments are disposed or changed

    • Regarding hydration and feeding

  • Encourage fluid intake and a healthy and balanced diet
