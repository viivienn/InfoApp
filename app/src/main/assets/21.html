If you are not confident about a transfer, DO NOT proceed. Ask for assistance.


Communication 
  • Make sure to communicate with the patient and caregivers throughout the transfer
  • When communicating with a patient, make sure to 
    • Be respectful 
    • Be clear
    • Be brief
    • Name the action and who has to do it
    • Be positive and encouraging

    • During transfer
  • Adjust the height of chairs/bed to facilitate the transfer, if needed
    • When the patient moves from a sitting position to a standing position, ensure the surface where the patient is seated is not too low
    • Feet should be flat on the floor and the height of the bed should be at the same level or higher than the knees when the patient is seated
  • Ensure the patient is not feeling any pain or discomfort during a movement 
    • Break down the movement in small step
    • Make sure to give instruction with simple words
    • If the patient is dizzy or confused, take your time with the patient before transfers
  • Touch the ends of patient’s fingers with your fingertip in order to stimulate them to stand
    • Could be useful for patient with limited cognitive abilities 



    • Check for the patient’s ability to help in transfers
  • Make sure the patient has proper attention (time of the day could affect)
    • E.g. Eye contact, verbal contact, “Mrs. X., Look at me”
  • Assess patient’s ability to use their arms/legs
    • E.g. Ask the patient for a firm handshake to evaluate their mobility and strength
  • Assess if the patient is able to maintain their balance and to sit without any support
