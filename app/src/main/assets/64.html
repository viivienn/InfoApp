Adapted clothing:
Often used for residents who are unable to dress themselves up
  • It is usually worn from the front and secured at the back of the resident with either velcro or buttons
  • For adapted pants, the velcro or buttons are usually on the sides.

    • How to dress a resident (shirt): Start by putting one sleeve of the shirt through the resident’s arm. If applicable, start with the weaker or injured side of the resident.
    • Put the sleeve through the resident’s arm to avoid moving it, as it may be painful for the resident
    If the resident is able to participate, encourage the resident to lean forward. Then, attach the two ends of the adapted clothing with the velcro or buttons. 

    If the resident does not have the proper strength to lean forward, gently bring their torso forward 
    How to undress a resident’s shirt: 
    1. Ask or assist the resident to lean forward in order to undo the velcro or the button of the adaptive clothing.
    2. Remove the sleeve of the adaptive clothing from the non-injured side of the resident first (if applicable)
      • Ask and/or assist the resident to lift their arm
    3. Remove the sleeve from the injured or weaker arm. 
      •   Make sure to not move their injured or weaker arm too much and be gentle.
