Walker
The height of the walker should be at the resident’s wrist level when standing. If it is not the case, ask for a professional.

Where to stand?
Stand one step behind the resident. Remain vigilant.

Verbal cues for walking with a walker
  • Take the first step with the walker
  • Put down the walker, making sure all 4 ends of the walker are on the ground
  • Take a step forward. The body and the feet of the resident should not be touching the walker.


    • Cane, Cane Tripod and Quad Cane
    The cane should be at the resident’s wrist level when standing straight. If it is not the case, ask a professional for help.
  • Usually, the resident should hold their cane on the side with their strongest leg

    • Where to stand?
  • Stand on the weaker side of the resident (opposite side of the cane) when they are walking, sitting or standing up.
  • The resident should not hold onto the cane while they’re standing up (risk of fall)

    • Verbal cues for walking with a cane
  • Take the first step with the canne and the opposite leg at the same time
  • Then take a step with the other leg
