Standing up and walking using a thumb hold 

1. Have the patient sitting on the side of the bed, adjust the height of the bed so the patient’s hips are higher than their knees

2. When holding the patient, make sure to stay close to the patient

3. Both caregivers and the patient take a step forward in sync. A signal could be given by one of the caregivers (a count to 3)

4. Make sure that the patient is stable when standing

4. Firmly grip the soaker pad

5. Transfer your weight from your front leg to the back leg to slide the patient towards the head of the bed

    Turning the patient’s on their side on the bed

    1. Before starting to reposition the patient, ensure:

  • Patient is bending their knees or crossing their legs. The crossed leg must be opposite to the side you want to turn the patient.
    • If you turn the patient to their left, cross their right leg over their left leg
  • The soaker pad is on the bed, under the patient
  • The bed is adjusted to the height of the caregiver’s hips
  • Both caregiver are standing face to each other on each side of the bed

    • ðŸ’¡ Reminder: bend your elbows slightly during the transfer for patient handling 

    2. During the transfer, if you want to turn the patient onto their left side:

    Caregiver 1: 
  • Slide the patient to the edge of the bed by pulling on the soaker pad, using a weight transfer from the front leg to the back leg (front-to-back). This could be done in several steps
  • Make sure the soaker pad stays under the patient.


    • Caregiver 2: 
  • Turn the patient towards you, onto their left side, using a front-to-back weight transfer


    • Caregiver 1:
  • After the patient is repositioned, stabilize them to make sure that they do not roll over. 
  • You may place a pillow against the patient’s back to help them maintain their new position
