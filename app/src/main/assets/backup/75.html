If the resident is acting anxious or distrustful:

  1. DO NOT try to reason or to discuss with the resident
  2. Attempt to distract the resident by changing the subject of the conversation to something they like or by proposing to do an activity that is enjoyable for them

For example: 
  • If the resident has a picture of their family in their room, ask for the names of the people that are in the photo
  • If the resident usually enjoys crossword puzzles, give them a puzzle to complete and come back later to finish your tasks

    If the resident is being violent or aggressive: 

    1. At the first signs of aggressivity, if the resident is not in any danger of falling, stop what you are doing to allow the resident to regain their calm 
      • This can take from a few seconds up to a few minutes

    2. Remain calm and keep reassuring the resident

    3. If the resident is not at risk of falling or in a medical emergency, ensure to leave them enough personal space. Leave the room if needed. 

    4. After the event, attempt to recall potential phrases or actions that might have caused the resident to become aggressive to prevent the same situation from happening again in the future
