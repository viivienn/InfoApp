This section includes a few positions that are often seen in people with reduced mobility in long-term care facilities. However, it does not encompass all positions. 


Supine position (resident lying on their back) for a short rest



  • Place a pillow under the head and shoulders
  • Arms can be 
    • Well supported with folded towels on either side OR
    • Slightly bent and placed on the body
  • Place one pillow under the thigh to slightly bend the knee
  • Heels are resting on the bed
    • This position is not recommended for periods over 30min

    Supine position (resident lying on their back) often used for sleeping



  • Head of the bed raised at 15°
  • Place a pillow under the head and shoulders 
  • Arms can be 
    • Well supported with folded towels on either side OR
    • Slightly bent and placed on the chest 
  • Raise the foot of the bed slightly (prevents resident from sliding down the bed)

    • Lateral position (resident lying on their side)



  • Place a pillow under the head and shoulders 
  • Lower arm should be forward 
    • Wrist can be supported with a rolled towel 
    • Avoid having the resident resting on top of their arm
  • Place a pillow under the upper arm to support it properly
  • The upper leg is bent to avoid it resting fully on the lower leg
    • If needed, place a pillow between the legs 
    • Flexed position may be more comfortable for the resident, but ensure to straighten the limbs after maximum 2h to avoid deformities and pain

    Example of a bad positioning:

  • The resident resting on top of their lower arm
  • The upper arm is not supported
